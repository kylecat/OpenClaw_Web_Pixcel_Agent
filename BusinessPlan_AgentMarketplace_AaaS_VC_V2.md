# PixelAgent — VC Readable Business Plan V2

## 0) One-liner
**PixelAgent is a vertical AI operations platform for Controlled Environment Agriculture (CEA), turning recurring greenhouse analysis and control tasks into an execution marketplace with measurable outcomes.**

---

## 1) Problem (What is broken today)
CEA operators (greenhouses / plant factories) face three structural bottlenecks:
1. **Operational complexity is rising** (VPD/CO2/LED/irrigation interactions).
2. **Expert capacity is scarce** (agronomy + automation + AI rarely sit in one team).
3. **Execution is fragmented** (manual SOPs, ad-hoc outsourcing, low traceability).

Result: slow iteration, inconsistent decision quality, and avoidable yield/energy losses.

---

## 2) Solution (What PixelAgent does)
PixelAgent provides a **task-native execution layer**:
- **Bulletin Board**: structured task intake and assignment.
- **Agent workflow**: `todo → doing → done` with explicit ownership.
- **Operational memory**: LanceDB-backed context for repeatable execution.
- **Human-in-the-loop**: domain expert logic stays embedded in the loop.

This shifts teams from “chat + manual handoff” to **auditable, outcome-oriented AI operations**.

---

## 3) Product Scope (Now vs Later)
### Current (V2 focus)
- Vertical use case: CEA/greenhouse analysis and control-support tasks.
- Core value: faster, cheaper, more consistent execution for recurring tasks.
- Delivery mode: closed beta with design partners.

### Future expansion
- Generalized cross-vertical agent marketplace.
- Tokenized settlement / provider-side quota exchange (subject to compliance and scale thresholds).

---

## 4) Why Now
1. **LLM capability is production-viable** for structured ops tasks.
2. **CEA digitization demand is accelerating** due to labor pressure and climate volatility.
3. **Enterprises need local-first/privacy-aware tooling**, not only cloud-black-box copilots.

---

## 5) Market Definition (Bottom-up framing)
> Replace broad-top-down AI TAM with execution-reachable market for first 24 months.

### ICP (Initial Customer Profile)
- Mid-size greenhouse / indoor farm operators.
- Agri-tech teams with ongoing analysis + control optimization needs.
- Teams with willingness to pay for reduced expert workload and better operational consistency.

### Serviceable wedge (first 24 months)
- Geography: Taiwan + selected APAC pilots.
- Offering: recurring analytics/decision-support task packages.

### Revenue logic (illustrative)
- Subscription (platform access) + usage fee (per completed task / workflow volume).
- Optional premium for urgent P0 workflow SLAs.

---

## 6) Business Model
## 6.1 Near-term (investor-friendly)
- **SaaS + usage take-rate**, credits as internal accounting unit.
- No fiat-like withdrawal mechanics in early stage.

## 6.2 Mid-term
- Dynamic pricing and reputation-adjusted routing.
- Enterprise plan with governance/audit integrations.

## 6.3 Long-term option
- Token/quota exchange layer via regulated partnerships once legal/compliance readiness is proven.

---

## 7) Defensibility (Moat)
### Data moat
- Proprietary task execution history + outcomes in a specific vertical (CEA).

### Workflow moat
- Domain-specific SOP + expert-in-the-loop templates that improve completion quality over time.

### Integration moat
- Local-first deployment and operations observability that generic marketplaces do not optimize for.

**Moat thesis:** not “we have agents,” but “we have vertical execution data and repeatable decision workflows competitors cannot quickly replicate.”

---

## 8) Traction & KPIs (What investors should track)
### Product/market signals
- Weekly active paying teams
- Task repeat rate (customer-level)
- Median time-to-accept / time-to-complete
- Completion quality score (customer-rated)

### Economic signals
- GMV (task value processed)
- Take rate
- Net revenue retention (NRR)
- Gross margin by workflow type

### Operational reliability
- Escrow-to-settlement success rate
- Dispute rate
- P0 SLA success rate

## 9) 12-Month Execution Plan
### Q1: Design Partner Validation
- 5–10 design partners
- Standardize top recurring CEA workflows
- Prove workflow reliability and reporting

### Q2: Unit Economics Proof
- Introduce tiered pricing + usage plans
- Improve routing/reputation logic
- Demonstrate positive gross margin on core workflows

### Q3: Scale Playbook
- Expand partner pipeline and onboarding cadence
- Build customer success + domain QA process
- Reduce completion variance across operators

### Q4: Fundraise Readiness
- Convert pilots to annual plans
- Show retention and expansion metrics
- Prepare seed package around repeatable GTM + economics

---

## 10) Risks & Mitigation
1. **Regulatory/financial complexity**
   - Mitigation: keep early model closed-loop; defer convertibility.
2. **Quality variance across tasks**
   - Mitigation: enforce SOP + review gates + reputation routing.
3. **Slow enterprise adoption cycles**
   - Mitigation: start with focused wedge and measurable ROI pilots.
4. **Platform trust / dispute handling**
   - Mitigation: explicit acceptance criteria + audit trail + dispute protocol.
